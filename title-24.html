Meeting California's 2045 Neutrality Goal

California’s state goal is to achieve 100% carbon neutrality by 2045. To keep building systems aligned with this target, the California Energy Commission (CEC) updates the Building Energy Efficiency Standards (Title 24, Part 6) every three years. The latest standards went into effect on **January 1, 2023**.

Under these rules, builders of new homes, commercial properties, and multi-family structures must incorporate clean solar generation. For commercial construction, **battery storage installations** are also mandated alongside solar.

Excite Energy acts as your compliance partner. We coordinate directly with general contractors and architectural firms, providing PE-stamped electrical plans that satisfy local building departments at the lowest possible equipment cost.

Commercial Solar Mandated Sectors

Under Title 24, new construction in the following building categories must install solar energy systems:

  • ✓ Medical Offices & Clinics

Solar PV Exemptions

  • ✕ SARA (Solar Access Roof Area) is less than 3% of Conditioned Floor Area.
  • ✕ Total contiguous SARA is less than 80 square feet.
  • ✕ The calculated required PV system is under 4 kW.
  • ✕ Roof structure prevents meeting local snow load regulations (ASCE 7-16).

Battery Exemptions

  • ✕ Required solar PV system size is less than 15% of Equation 140.10-A.
  • ✕ Usable capacity requirement is under 10 kWh.
  • ✕ Single-tenant building is under 5,000 sq ft of conditioned area.
  • ✕ Building is in California Climate Zone 1 (Northern Coast).
Compliance Mathematics

Title 24 Sizing Equations

The California Energy Commission mandates sizing based on building footprint variables.

Equation 140.10-A: Photovoltaic Direct Current Size

kW_PV_DC = (CFA × A) / 1000
  • kW_PV_DC = Minimum size of the PV system in kW.
  • CFA = Conditioned Floor Area in square feet.
  • A = PV capacity factor specified in CEC Table 140.10-A (varies by building type and climate zone).

Equation 140.10-B: Battery Storage Usable Energy Capacity

kWh_batt = kW_PV_dc × B / D^0.5
  • kWh_batt = Usable energy capacity of the battery system in kWh.
  • kW_PV_dc = Required PV system capacity in kW (calculated in 140.10-A).
  • B = Battery energy capacity factor from CEC Table 140.10-B.
  • D = Rated round-trip AC-to-AC cycle efficiency of the battery storage.

Equation 140.10-C: Battery Storage Rated Power Capacity

kW_batt = kW_PV_dc × C
  • kW_batt = Minimum rated power output of the battery storage system in kW.
  • C = Battery power capacity factor specified in Table 140.10-B for the building type.
Cost Reduction Innovation

Reduce Cost via Decentralized Battery Storage

If your goal is to satisfy Title 24 mandates with the lowest upfront capital expenditure, **decentralized modular storage** is often the best solution. Many contractors only design centralized storage systems, which require concrete pads, heavy fire suppression plumbing, and central HVAC containers. Decentralized systems (like Yotta Energy) mount directly under individual rooftop panels, eliminating massive equipment footprints and lowering installation costs by up to 35%.
