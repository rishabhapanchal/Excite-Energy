Mounting Options

Every solar array is only as secure as the brackets holding it down. We engineer robust, leak-free mounts for every roof and site profile.

We install solar arrays on roofs, ground structures, pergolas, and carports. Each mounting style utilizes specific certified hardware to ensure high wind and seismic stability.

Option 1

Ground Mounts

Ground-mounted solar arrays are an excellent solution when there is available land near the property. They offer key benefits:

  • Optimal Azimuth: Panels can be oriented exactly south for maximum daily generation.
  • Cooler Operation: Airflow underneath prevents heat build-up, increasing panel efficiency.
  • Simple Maintenance: Washing off dry weather dust is quick and safe.
  • Considerations: Requires trenching conduit at least 18 inches deep, avoiding leach fields/septic systems.
Option 2

Concrete Tile Roofs

Concrete tile roofing is highly common in the Sacramento Valley. We ensure complete water sealing through a careful, multi-step process:

  • Remove individual tiles above building rafters.
  • Drill pilot hole into rafters, fill with high-grade tripolymer sealant.
  • Bolt a heavy-duty metal hook down directly to the roof structure.
  • Grind a slot on the back of the concrete tile if necessary so it sits flat.
  • Avoid cheap "drill-through-tile" shortcuts that compromise roof water barriers.
Option 3

Clay Tile Roofs

Clay tiles are fragile and break easily under installer weight. To prevent future leaks, we utilize an "inlaid shingle transition" method:

  • Remove clay tiles in the designated solar array footprint.
  • Lay down high-durability composite shingles in that space.
  • Attach the solar racking securely directly through the shingle underlayment.
  • Re-install the clay tiles to border the solar panels.
  • Delivers a beautiful, inlaid look while preserving roof waterproofing.
Option 4

Corrugated & Standing Seam Metal

Metal roofing represents one of the strongest, most stable attachment bases for solar installations:

  • Standing Seam: Utilize special clamps (like S-5!) that grip the vertical metal seam, requiring zero roof penetrations.
  • Corrugated Metal: Use brackets with built-in EPDM rubber grommets that seal directly to the metal crown.
  • Superior strength and extremely low load-weight per square foot.
Option 5

Tilt Kits & Flat Roofs

When roof surfaces face away from optimal solar angles (such as steep northern slopes), we use tilt-up racking systems:

  • Corrects angles on flat or low-pitch roofs to hit standard southern exposures.
  • Increases annual electricity production by up to 25% on poorly oriented roofs.
  • Requires structural calculation to ensure wind lift rating is maintained.
Option 6

Shade Structures (Carports & Pergolas)

We build and install solar arrays on custom carports or wood pergolas, turning shade space into an active power plant:

  • Requires detailed structural engineering plans for building departments.
  • Permit validation is required for all supporting concrete footers, rebar, and support columns.
  • Perfect option when roof space is shaded by trees or ground mount space is unavailable.
Commercial Flat Roofs

Membrane & Single-Ply Roofing

For flat commercial roofs, we offer two main racking systems: **Ballasted Systems** and **Attached Systems**. Ballasted racking uses heavy concrete block anchors, eliminating membrane penetrations. For sloped membranes or seismic zones, we install membrane-specific attached brackets wrapped in custom boots to prevent any moisture ingress.
